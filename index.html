How it works

From confusion
to clarity in minutes

You upload your evidence. NoCase does the rest — simultaneously building the strongest possible case for both sides while a judge evaluates the evidence in real time.

01
Choose your side
Tell us if you are the plaintiff bringing a claim, the defendant responding to one, or neutral. Have an existing case number? We'll pull the public court record automatically.
02
Upload your evidence
Drag and drop contracts, emails, bank statements, photos, PDFs — anything relevant. Forward emails directly to cases@nocase.org with your case ID in the subject line.
03
AI argues both sides
The prosecution panel builds the strongest case against you. The defense panel builds your strongest defense. Both update in real time as you add more evidence.
04
Judge renders verdict
The center panel acts as judge — evaluating both sides and rendering a running verdict with a confidence score. Starts at "No Case" and builds as evidence accumulates.
05
Find a lawyer
When you are ready, NoCase generates a professional case brief and delivers it to matching attorneys in your jurisdiction. You pay only when an attorney accepts your case.

Privacy architecture

Designed so we
cannot betray you.

A subpoena served on Benchmark produces nothing useful — because we designed the architecture so we technically cannot access your case data.

What we cannot produce
Your uploaded documents — encrypted on your device before transmission. We store encrypted blobs we cannot read.
Your AI analysis — prosecution arguments, defense arguments, and judge verdicts are encrypted with your key.
Your email communications — forwarded emails are encrypted at rest and in transit under your case key.
Your identity linkage — your encryption key is never stored on our servers. We cannot connect you to your case data.
What survives deletion
Blockchain time records — anonymized case ID hash, session timestamps, duration. No content. No parties. No details.
Deletion confirmation — proof that a case existed and was deleted on a specific date. Required for legal compliance.
Billing records — number of AI calls made for invoicing purposes only. No case content referenced.
Copies you made outside NoCase — we cannot control documents you downloaded or sent to others. That is your responsibility.

What we collect

When you create a case on NoCase, Benchmark collects: (a) your NoCase ID and hashed PIN — we never store your PIN in plain text; (b) encrypted case data — documents, analysis, and communications encrypted with your key, which we cannot read; (c) session metadata — timestamps, duration, and action types recorded to the blockchain and our servers in anonymized form; (d) billing information — if you subscribe to a paid plan, payment is processed by our payment processor and we do not store your full payment card information.

What we cannot read

Due to client-side encryption, Benchmark cannot read: your uploaded documents; your AI-generated analysis; your email communications forwarded to your case; or any content associated with your case. We store encrypted data only.

How we use what we collect

Session metadata is used to: maintain the blockchain time record for your case; calculate billing for paid plans; improve platform performance; and comply with legal obligations. We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers. We do not use your case data to train AI models.

We retain your encrypted case data until you delete it. Upon deletion, all encrypted data is permanently destroyed from our servers within 24 hours. Your right to delete

You have the unconditional right to permanently delete your NoCase case and all associated data at any time, without reason, from within the platform. Deletion is immediate and irreversible.

What is deleted

  • All uploaded documents and files — permanently destroyed from S3 storage
  • All AI-generated analysis — prosecution arguments, defense arguments, judge verdicts
  • All email communications forwarded to your case
  • Your encryption key fragments — permanently breaking decryption
  • All case records from our database
  • Your PIN hash — meaning the case can never be reopened

What is not deleted

  • Blockchain time records — anonymized case ID hash, session timestamps, and duration. These are immutable by design and contain no case content.

  • Billing records — required by law for seven years for tax and accounting purposes.

FAQ

Common questions.

Can Benchmark be subpoenaed for my case data? +
A subpoena served on Benchmark would produce only encrypted data we cannot read, anonymized blockchain timestamps with no content, and a deletion record if you deleted your case. We cannot produce your documents or analysis because we technically do not have access to them — they are encrypted with a key we do not hold.
Is NoCase practicing law? +
No. Benchmark provides legal information — the same way a law library or legal website does — not legal advice. The AI generates analysis for informational and strategic purposes. It does not advise you on what to do, recommend a legal strategy for your specific circumstances, or create any attorney-client relationship. We display this disclaimer prominently on every analysis output.
What happens if the AI cites a case that doesn't exist? +
AI systems can hallucinate case citations. Every analysis NoCase produces includes a mandatory verification notice that all citations must be checked through Westlaw or LexisNexis before any reliance. Do not file any document with a court based on NoCase citations without independent verification. Your attorney is responsible for verifying any citations in filed documents.
Who owns the case brief sent to attorneys? +
You do. The brief is generated from your uploaded documents and is your property. By generating a brief and requesting attorney referral, you authorize NoCase to deliver it to selected attorneys in your jurisdiction on your behalf. Attorneys receive the brief for evaluation purposes only and have no right to use it for any purpose other than evaluating whether to accept your matter.
What if I delete my case but have an active court obligation to preserve evidence? +
This is your responsibility, not Benchmark's. If you have received a litigation hold notice, been served with discovery, or have any court-ordered obligation to preserve evidence, do not delete your case without consulting your attorney first. Benchmark displays a warning when deletion is attempted for cases with an existing case number, but the final decision and legal responsibility is yours.
Can a minor use NoCase? +
No. NoCase is for users 18 and older only. Our terms of service prohibit use by minors. If you are under 18 with a legal matter, please speak with a parent or guardian who can consult with an attorney on your behalf.
Does NoCase work for cases outside Texas? +
NoCase currently works best for Texas civil, criminal, and real estate matters where our AI has the strongest jurisdiction-specific training. You can select other states and federal courts during onboarding, but our analysis is most detailed for Texas. We are expanding jurisdiction coverage regularly. All analyses are clearly labeled with the selected jurisdiction.
